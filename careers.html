Financial Analyst

Career Introduction

Financial analysts evaluate business performance, study market trends, and help companies make investment decisions. They use data, reports, and forecasting models to guide financial planning and growth strategies.

Salary Info:

$65,000 – $110,000 per year (entry to mid-level in the U.S.)

Work Hours

40–50 hours per week, can increase during earnings season or deadlines

Key Skills

  • Excel & financial modeling
  • Data analysis
  • Critical thinking
  • Accounting fundamentals
  • Attention to detail

Education

Bachelor’s degree in Finance, Economics, Accounting, or Business

Data Analyst

Career Introduction

Data analysts collect, clean, and interpret large sets of data to help organizations make better decisions. They turn raw information into charts, dashboards, and insights used in business strategy.

Salary Info:

$60,000 – $100,000 per year

Work Hours

40 hours per week (typically standard office schedule)

Key Skills

  • SQL & databases
  • Excel / Google Sheets
  • Data visualization (Tableau, Power BI)
  • Problem solving
  • Basic programming (Python helpful)

Education

Bachelor’s degree in Data Science, Computer Science, Statistics, or related field

Financial Analyst

Career Introduction

Financial advisors help individuals manage money, plan for retirement, and make investment decisions. They build long-term client relationships and provide personalized financial guidance.

Salary Info:

$55,000 – $120,000+ (often commission-based, varies widely)

Work Hours

40–55 hours per week, flexible depending on client meetings

Key Skills

  • Communication & people skills
  • Investment knowledge
  • Sales & persuasion
  • Financial planning
  • Trust-building and ethics

Education

Bachelor’s degree in Finance, Business, Economics, or related field
